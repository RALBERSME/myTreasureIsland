Almost all people suffer from feelings of inferiority to some degree.



Very few people have the impression that they are enough as they are. The idea of ​​not being enough is a belief that has been developed based on experiences with the environment, but of which the individual is often not even aware. For example, people believe they are "too fat", "too stupid", "too old", "too young", "too naive", "too inexperienced", "too..." without even questioning the correctness of this belief.

We believe that so-called "successful" people have extreme self-confidence, but it is precisely these people who always want to be in the spotlight and always have to be the best who often suffer massively from a lack of self-confidence and only try to conceal it.

Self-confidence is an essential issue, especially for people who have experienced rejection or exclusion as children or who have not been able to establish a secure bond with their parents.
Toxic relationships are usually about deliberately undermining the other person’s self-confidence through manipulative methods.

The more self-worth is sought externally, the greater the self-insecurity and associated inferiority when the external confirmation expected is not forthcoming.

Confidence and Self Belief Statistics UK 2021 - 2022
Key Findings Women: Self Confidence Statistics

2,000 UK adults were surveyed in February 2021 using the research company Censuswide. The key findings revealed that:

A staggering 85% of women do not believe they are attractive.
Only 23% of women say they feel confident in their appearance,
meaning that 4 in every 5 women do not feel confident in the way they look.
Almost two thirds of women are not confident in their ability to do their job.
The majority of women do not believe they are intelligent (62%) More than half of women do not think they are liked by others (56%)

Signs of a lack of self-confidence:

  • Courting for attention
  • Focusing on weaknesses
  • Envy/resentment of other people's successes
  • Postponing decisions
  • Comparing yourself to others
  • Avoiding confrontations
  • Making hasty judgments instead of acceptance
  • Not being able to listen
  • Perceiving other people's opinions as a personal attack
  • Not being able to accept compliments out of shame
  • Striving for perfection as a defense mechanism





Feelings of inferiority are therefore human. But that does not mean that you have to accept them without question or even suffer from them.

Self-confident people are characterized by deep satisfaction with themselves and their lives.






To achieve this goal of self-confidence and self-esteem, the following aspects are beneficial:


  • Be aware of your own strengths and weaknesses
  • Focus on your strengths
  • Accept your weaknesses
  • See mistakes as learning steps
  • Be aware of your own abilities
  • Organize your life according to your abilities
  • Free yourself from negative past experiences
  • Stop criticizing yourself
  • Do not take criticism from others personally
  • Question whether criticism is justified
  • Focus on goals that fulfill you
  • Look for an environment that is well-disposed towards you and supports you
  • Avoid toxic people
  • Look at what successes you have already achieved
  • Give yourself rewards for achieving goals
  • Instead of "I can't do that", think from now on: "I can't do that YET, but I can learn"
  • Instead of "I must", think from now on: "I want to"
  • Recognize your own needs and value them; communicate them to those around you
  • Set clear boundaries to protect your needs
  • Adopt an upright, self-confident posture
