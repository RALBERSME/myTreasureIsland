• Verena Kast - the grieving process when someone is lost

  • grieving process phase I
    (Verena Kast)

    Not wanting to believe it: The loss is denied, the grieving person usually feels numb and is often paralyzed with horror.

  • grieving process phase II
    (Verena Kast)

    In the second phase, sadness, anger, joy, rage, fear and restlessness are experienced in a mixed manner, and the search for someone to “guilty” may begin.

  • grieving process phase III
    (Verena Kast)

    In the third phase of grief, the lost person is “searched for” unconsciously or consciously.

  • grieving process phase IV
    (Verena Kast)

    In the fourth phase, the loss is accepted to such an extent that the lost person has become an internal figure. Possibilities in life that were achieved through the relationship and that were previously only possible within the relationship can now partly become possibilities of one's own.
